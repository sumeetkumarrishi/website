Important LLM Benchmarks

Category: AI → Models

Overview

Benchmarks measure different capabilities of a model — science knowledge, reasoning, mathematics, coding, and frontier intelligence. No single benchmark tells the whole story, so it helps to look across several.

GPQA Science

Google-Proof Q&A — graduate-level science questions across Physics, Chemistry, and Biology, designed to stay hard even when people can search the web.

SolverApprox. score
Non-PhD humans (with web access)~34%
PhD-level experts~65%

MMLU-Pro Reasoning

The successor to MMLU, built to be a tougher and cleaner test of general reasoning:

  • Harder questions across many subjects
  • 10 answer choices instead of 4
  • Reduced ambiguity

Because it is harder and less ambiguous than the original MMLU, companies still report it as a strong signal of general reasoning ability.

AIME Mathematics

A competitive mathematics benchmark, useful for evaluating:

  • Reasoning models
  • Problem solving
  • Mathematical thinking

LiveCodeBench Coding

Measures coding ability, algorithmic thinking, and problem solving.

  • LeetCode-style questions
  • Frequently updated tasks to avoid training-data contamination

MuSR Multi-step

A murder-mystery benchmark for multi-step reasoning. The model receives:

  • A 1000-word story
  • Multiple suspects

The question: who had motive, means, and opportunity?

This is much easier for readers to understand than abstract benchmarks — it tests reasoning in a way anyone can follow.

Humanity's Last Exam (HLE) Frontier

  • Created because older benchmarks became too easy
  • 2,500 extremely difficult questions
  • Designed to measure frontier intelligence
